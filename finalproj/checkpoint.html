Final Project: Adjustable Liquid Simulation

Amy Jiang, Christopher Thomas, Jeremy Ahn, Jim Kang

2D simulation for various liquids with differing properties that may result in different fluid movement/ interaction with solid objects in simulation. Able to observe how the drop of solid mass affects the fluid movement of different liquids.

Project Milestone Update

So far, we have conducted research pertaining to how to model fluids as a grid of particle point masses. To accurately render water (and other liquids), we plan to abide by the following three methods:

  • Procedural water: “animates a physical effect directly instead of simulating the cause of it” (Bridson and Muller-Fischer 59)
  • Heightfield Approximation: “surface is represented as a two dimensional function or heightfield and animated using the 2-d wave equation” (Bridson and Muller-Fischer 59).
    • We can use this algorithm to quickly and efficiently simulate bodies of water
  • Particle Systems: “small amount of water, a splashing fluid, a jet of water, small puddles or runnels” (Bridson and Muller-Fischer 59)
    • Lennard-Jones Force: Often used in molecular dynamic simulations, we would use this equation to simulate small bodies of water with particles and how each particle interacts with other particles
